# Gap: how much clear floor a bathroom door swing needs before the layout counts as usable, not blocked

- **Domain:** interior-design
- **Type:** fit
- **Status:** accumulating
- **Origin:** seed (interior-design had no prior coverage; user's en-suite scenario was the domain's first entry)
- **Closure condition:** a second, independent session reproduces a compatible swing-clearance-vs-fixture layout, or a human confirms the rule during review

## Sessions

### 2026-07-10 · teacher
- **Scenario / mad-lib:** user was put in charge of an en-suite bathroom off a bedroom — sink, toilet, shower, a door with a clear path to open that must not swing into the bedroom
- **User inputs:** door swings outward away from the bedroom; door width "whatever a bathroom door is" (resolved via research: 30–34", NKBA min clear opening 32" → 34" door, 36" for accessibility)
- **Artifacts:** 2 floor-plan sketches (raster) — iteration 1 placed the toilet so its clearance zone overlapped the swing arc (conflict); iteration 2 moved the sink beside the door and the toilet clear of the arc (resolved)
- **Steer / outcome:** iteration 1 flagged the overlap; iteration 2 confirmed no fixture-clearance/swing overlap and no bedroom-side swing → session converged at iteration 2 of 5
- **Mined:** "clearance is sized to the fixture layout, not fixed by the bedroom-side swing alone; an individual-use bathroom may let the door swing into clear floor space beyond the arc if fixtures' required zones stay clear" — provenance: Verified (ADA/IRC/NKBA sources)
- **Left open:** only one session, one footprint — the rule needs an independent session on a different room shape before it can close
